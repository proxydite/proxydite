a commitment against digital technology

I am a deeply technological being. From a young age my fingers have input data into machines and those machines returned vast amounts of information to me. History, art, memes, the social lives of my peers and pornography have been readily available to me for much of my life. I have taken this access of technology and made a career out of making moving images digitally that I then project on stages, buildings and objects. Augmenting the world with technology is my craft and a vocation I was heading towards from the first time I turned on a PC. My graduation project at University was an exploration of how AI would impact our lives, how it was essential that humanity remained at the core of these new technological developments and how a world was possible where we would be ‘all watched over by machines of loving grace’. I realise now that I was wrong.

Interconnected digital technology, whilst profound in it’s ability to give us access to knowledge, to the world and to each other, is a spiritually corrupt and inadequate means of advancing humanity. It is a network of harmful energy that allows evil to spread throughout our communities and ourselves. In social media we have allowed no less than the forces of hell directly into our homes, with access to our innermost thoughts and desires. I don't talk of the worldly men and women who have created these platforms, often in earnest and with a deep desire for social good, but instead believe that dark forces have infiltrated these technologies and in doing so have usurped their power for ill will.

There is of course value in technology. The scythe and the hammer are technologies, so to are the tractor and the train. Indeed, I'm not necessarily railing against the personal computer which is enabling me to write my prose, check for errors and publish this to the world. One's use of a machine is his or her responsibility. Your free will determining how you use it. If one wishes to write an essay discussing the dangers of technology, that is a choice. If one wishes to post explicit images of themselves online, that is a choice. If one wishes to find bomb making recipes online, that is a choice. Choices have responsibility and consequences. That is down to the individual to control. However, when we connect these machines to one another we form a network that is far beyond our control.

Of course we can set IP addresses, route traffic through specific servers and establish domains, the infrastructure is certainly under our control. Yet the energy (and I'm not talking about electricity here) is something that is it's own being. It flows through each of us, tickling our dopamine receptors and tapping into our deepest darkest thoughts and making us victims of instinct and reaction. It is clear that at the heart of death of culture in the West is interconnected digital technology that has sought to homogenise vast swathes of our lives. It has permeated through much of the fabric of society from the music we listen to, the films that we watch and the way in which we interact.

There is an *almost* universal acknowledgement of the evil of social media for example, and yet many who espouse it's dangers still regularly post on it's many platforms often claiming it to be 'essential' to their work and therefore their livelihood. We have surrendered to the seductive power of ease. We have become shackled to it, dependent on it. Simply put, we have become addicted to it. We are the fodder that feeds the beast that seeks to divide us and push humanity towards it's destruction.

I will no longer participate in this. I denounce 'artificial intelligence' and the coming onslaught it will bring of both labour and lives. I denounce the further centralisation of technology and society that will further entrench the globalist elite who seek to control every aspect of our lives. I denounce those who seek to divide us with 'politics' which is the invention of the Devil. My social media pages will remain active but I will no longer use them with the exception of sharing updates to my website. I will prioritise real world experiences and make a commitment to building resilient communities on the ground away from the ensnaring allure of the global tech elite.

"God is spirit, and those who worship him must worship in spirit and truth." John 4:24
