Rhyddid

Rhyddid was a heartfelt video installation that brought five pivotal moments in Welsh history to life. At its core was an emotional interview with a survivor of the Aberfan disaster, paired with stories like Eileen Beasley’s fight for the Welsh language and the Llanelli Riots’ call for justice. The films were projected onto a large wooden cutout of Wales, flanked by panels that formed a striking, immersive screen. This blend of light, storytelling, and raw history connected viewers to the resilience and spirit of Wales.

Simul

Simul was my first venture into VR, designed in Unreal Engine, and a milestone in my digital art journey. This immersive experience invited participants to explore a virtual world of tranquil, interactive natural spaces. Spanning three levels, each crafted to evoke calm and freedom, Simul offered a deeply personal connection to a world beyond reality—a place where exploration was as much about the self as the surroundings.
